# 90. Formatting Professional Notifications

Canonical documentation for 90. Formatting Professional Notifications. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 90. Formatting Professional Notifications exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of formatting professional notifications is to effectively communicate information to the intended audience in a clear, concise, and respectful manner. The problem space includes the need to convey complex information, ensure readability, and maintain a professional tone, while also being mindful of the audience's time and attention.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Formatting professional notifications involves considering the context, content, and presentation of the message. It requires an understanding of the audience, the purpose of the notification, and the desired outcome. A well-formatted notification should be easy to read, understand, and act upon, while also reflecting the professionalism and brand of the organization.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Notification | A message or alert that informs the recipient of an event, update, or request |
| Formatting | The process of arranging and presenting content in a clear and readable manner |
| Professional Tone | A tone that is respectful, courteous, and free of jargon or slang |
| Audience | The intended recipient of the notification, which may include customers, employees, or stakeholders |
| Call-to-Action | A prompt or instruction that encourages the recipient to take a specific action |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of formatting professional notifications include:
* Clarity: The message should be easy to understand and free of ambiguity
* Conciseness: The message should be brief and to the point, avoiding unnecessary detail
* Respect: The tone should be professional and courteous, avoiding jargon or slang
* Readability: The presentation should be easy to read, with clear headings, bullet points, and white space
* Consistency: The formatting and tone should be consistent throughout the notification

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for formatting professional notifications includes:
* A clear and concise subject line or heading
* A brief introduction or summary of the purpose of the notification
* A clear and concise body that provides the necessary information
* A call-to-action or prompt that encourages the recipient to take a specific action
* A professional closing or signature that includes contact information

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns for formatting professional notifications include:
* Using a standard template or format for consistency
* Including a clear and concise summary or introduction
* Using bullet points or numbered lists to present complex information
* Including a call-to-action or prompt to encourage the recipient to take action
* Using a professional tone and language throughout the notification

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns for formatting professional notifications include:
* Using jargon or slang that may be unfamiliar to the recipient
* Including unnecessary or redundant information
* Using a tone that is overly casual or informal
* Failing to include a clear call-to-action or prompt
* Using a format or presentation that is difficult to read or understand
