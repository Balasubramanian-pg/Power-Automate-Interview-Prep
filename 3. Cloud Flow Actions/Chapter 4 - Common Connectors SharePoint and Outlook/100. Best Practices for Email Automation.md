# 100. Best Practices for Email Automation

Canonical documentation for 100. Best Practices for Email Automation. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 100. Best Practices for Email Automation exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of 100. Best Practices for Email Automation is to provide a comprehensive guide for organizations to streamline their email communications, enhance customer engagement, and improve overall email marketing efficiency. The class of problems it addresses includes inefficient manual email processes, low email open rates, inadequate personalization, and poor email deliverability.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Email automation refers to the use of software and technologies to automate and optimize email marketing campaigns, allowing businesses to send targeted, personalized, and timely emails to their customers and prospects. This involves designing and implementing automated workflows, triggers, and rules to manage email sending, tracking, and analysis.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Email Automation | The use of software and technologies to automate and optimize email marketing campaigns. |
| Trigger | An event or action that initiates an automated email workflow. |
| Workflow | A series of automated actions and decisions that manage email sending, tracking, and analysis. |
| Personalization | The process of tailoring email content and messaging to individual recipients based on their preferences, behaviors, and demographics. |
| Deliverability | The ability of an email to reach the recipient's inbox, rather than being blocked or filtered as spam. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of email automation include:
* Automation workflows: designing and implementing automated processes to manage email sending and tracking.
* Personalization: using data and analytics to tailor email content and messaging to individual recipients.
* Segmentation: dividing email lists into targeted groups based on demographics, behaviors, and preferences.
* Triggered emails: sending automated emails in response to specific events or actions, such as welcome emails or abandoned cart reminders.
* Analytics and tracking: monitoring email performance and using data to optimize future campaigns.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for email automation involves the following components:
* Email service provider (ESP): a platform that manages email sending, tracking, and analysis.
* Marketing automation platform (MAP): a platform that automates and optimizes marketing workflows, including email.
* Customer relationship management (CRM) system: a platform that manages customer data and interactions.
* Data and analytics: tools and processes for collecting, analyzing, and using data to optimize email campaigns.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in email automation include:
* Welcome emails: automated emails sent to new subscribers or customers.
* Abandoned cart reminders: automated emails sent to customers who have left items in their shopping cart.
* Birthday and anniversary emails: automated emails sent to customers on their birthday or anniversary.
* Newsletter and promotional emails: regular emails sent to subscribers with news, updates, and promotions.
* Win-back emails: automated emails sent to inactive customers to re-engage them.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in email automation include:
* Spamming: sending unsolicited or irrelevant emails to recipients.
* Over-emailing: sending too many emails to recipients, leading to fatigue and decreased engagement.
* Lack of personalization: sending generic, non-targeted emails to recipients.
* Poor subject lines and email copy: using unengaging or misleading subject lines and email copy.
* Ignoring analytics and tracking: failing to monitor and use data to optimize email campaigns.
