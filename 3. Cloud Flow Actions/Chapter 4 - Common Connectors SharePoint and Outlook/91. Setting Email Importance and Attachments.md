# 91. Setting Email Importance and Attachments

Canonical documentation for 91. Setting Email Importance and Attachments. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 91. Setting Email Importance and Attachments exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of setting email importance and attachments is to enable effective communication and information exchange through email. This feature addresses the problem of conveying the urgency and significance of email messages, as well as the need to share files and documents with recipients. By setting email importance, senders can draw attention to critical messages, while attachments facilitate the exchange of supporting materials.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
The conceptual model of setting email importance and attachments involves understanding the components of an email message, including the subject, body, importance level, and attachments. Importance levels, such as high, normal, or low, indicate the urgency or significance of the message. Attachments, which can be files, images, or other documents, provide additional context or supporting information.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Email Importance | A designation indicating the level of urgency or significance of an email message, such as high, normal, or low. |
| Attachment | A file, image, or document sent with an email message to provide additional context or supporting information. |
| MIME (Multipurpose Internet Mail Extensions) | A standard for formatting email messages to include attachments and other multimedia content. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of setting email importance and attachments include understanding email protocols, such as SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol) and MIME, as well as the importance of clear subject lines, concise message bodies, and relevant attachments. Additionally, considerations such as file size limits, attachment types, and email client compatibility are essential for effective email communication.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for setting email importance and attachments involves using email clients or applications that support MIME and SMTP protocols. This model typically includes features such as:
* Importance level selection (high, normal, low)
* Attachment upload and management
* File type filtering and validation
* Email client configuration for optimal performance and compatibility

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns for setting email importance and attachments include:
* Using high importance for urgent or time-sensitive messages
* Including relevant attachments, such as documents or images, to support the email message
* Using clear and descriptive subject lines and message bodies
* Respecting file size limits and attachment type restrictions

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns for setting email importance and attachments include:
* Overusing high importance designations, leading to desensitization
* Sending unnecessary or large attachments, which can cause email client performance issues
* Using vague or misleading subject lines and message bodies
* Ignoring file type and size restrictions, potentially causing email delivery issues
