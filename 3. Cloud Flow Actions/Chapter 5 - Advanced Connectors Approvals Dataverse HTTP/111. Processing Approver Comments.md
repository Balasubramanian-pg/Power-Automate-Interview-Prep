# 111. Processing Approver Comments

Canonical documentation for 111. Processing Approver Comments. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 111. Processing Approver Comments exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of 111. Processing Approver Comments is to provide a standardized framework for managing and incorporating feedback from approvers in a structured and efficient manner. This addresses the problem of ensuring that all relevant stakeholders have a clear understanding of the approval process and that their comments are properly documented, tracked, and addressed.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
The conceptual model of 111. Processing Approver Comments involves a iterative cycle of comment submission, review, and resolution. Approvers submit comments, which are then reviewed and prioritized by the responsible team. The team addresses the comments, and the approvers verify that their concerns have been adequately resolved. This cycle repeats until all comments have been satisfactorily addressed.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Approver | An individual or entity responsible for reviewing and approving a document, process, or system. |
| Comment | A feedback or suggestion provided by an approver regarding a document, process, or system. |
| Comment Status | The current state of a comment, such as "Open", "In Progress", or "Resolved". |
| Comment Type | The category or classification of a comment, such as "Minor", "Major", or "Critical". |
| Resolution | The outcome or result of addressing a comment, such as "Accepted", "Rejected", or "Deferred". |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of 111. Processing Approver Comments include:
* **Comment Management**: The process of tracking, reviewing, and resolving comments in a structured and efficient manner.
* **Approval Workflow**: The sequence of steps involved in obtaining approval from approvers, including comment submission, review, and resolution.
* **Stakeholder Engagement**: The process of communicating with and involving approvers and other stakeholders throughout the comment management process.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for 111. Processing Approver Comments involves the following steps:
1. **Comment Submission**: Approvers submit comments through a designated channel or system.
2. **Comment Review**: The responsible team reviews and prioritizes the comments.
3. **Comment Resolution**: The team addresses the comments and provides a resolution.
4. **Comment Verification**: The approvers verify that their comments have been satisfactorily resolved.
5. **Comment Closure**: The comment is closed and marked as resolved.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in 111. Processing Approver Comments include:
* **Iterative Comment Management**: Repeatedly soliciting and incorporating feedback from approvers throughout the development or review process.
* **Collaborative Comment Resolution**: Involving multiple stakeholders in the comment resolution process to ensure that all concerns are addressed.
* **Transparent Comment Tracking**: Providing clear visibility into the comment management process, including comment status and resolution.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in 111. Processing Approver Comments include:
* **Comment Ignoring**: Failing to address or respond to comments from approvers.
* **Comment Hoarding**: Withholding or delaying comment submission, review, or resolution.
* **Lack of Transparency**: Failing to provide clear visibility into the comment management process or comment status.
