# 110. Handling Approval Outcomes

Canonical documentation for 110. Handling Approval Outcomes. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 110. Handling Approval Outcomes exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of 110. Handling Approval Outcomes is to provide a standardized approach to managing the outcomes of approval processes, ensuring that all stakeholders are informed and that subsequent actions are triggered accordingly. This topic addresses the class of problems related to approval workflow management, including inconsistent approval handling, lack of transparency, and inefficient communication.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Handling Approval Outcomes involves a structured approach to managing the results of approval processes, including the capture, processing, and communication of approval decisions. This encompasses the entire lifecycle of an approval, from initial submission to final disposition, and includes the various states and transitions that an approval may undergo.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Approval | A formal decision-making process where an individual or group assesses and decides on a proposal, request, or submission. |
| Approval Outcome | The result of an approval process, which may be approved, rejected, pending, or require revisions. |
| Approval State | The current status of an approval, such as submitted, in-review, approved, or rejected. |
| Approval Transition | The change from one approval state to another, triggered by an approval outcome or other event. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of Handling Approval Outcomes include:
* **Approval Workflow**: The sequence of steps and states that an approval undergoes, from submission to final disposition.
* **Approval Decision**: The outcome of an approval process, which may be approved, rejected, or require revisions.
* **Notification and Communication**: The process of informing stakeholders of approval outcomes and subsequent actions.
* **Audit and Tracking**: The maintenance of a record of all approval-related activities, including submissions, decisions, and transitions.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for Handling Approval Outcomes involves the following components:
* **Approval Submission**: The initial step in the approval process, where a proposal, request, or submission is captured and recorded.
* **Approval Review**: The assessment and evaluation of the submission, resulting in an approval decision.
* **Approval Decision**: The outcome of the approval review, which may be approved, rejected, or require revisions.
* **Notification and Communication**: The process of informing stakeholders of the approval outcome and subsequent actions.
* **Audit and Tracking**: The maintenance of a record of all approval-related activities.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in Handling Approval Outcomes include:
* **Serial Approval**: A linear approval process, where each approval decision is sequential and dependent on the previous outcome.
* **Parallel Approval**: A concurrent approval process, where multiple approvals are processed simultaneously and independently.
* **Conditional Approval**: An approval process that is dependent on specific conditions or criteria being met.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in Handling Approval Outcomes include:
* **Lack of Transparency**: Failing to provide clear and timely communication of approval outcomes and subsequent actions.
* **Inconsistent Approval Handling**: Applying inconsistent or subjective criteria to approval decisions, resulting in unfair or biased outcomes.
* **Insufficient Audit and Tracking**: Failing to maintain a complete and accurate record of approval-related activities.
