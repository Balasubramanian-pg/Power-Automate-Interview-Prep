# 101. Approvals Connector Overview

Canonical documentation for 101. Approvals Connector Overview. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
The 101. Approvals Connector Overview exists to provide a standardized framework for automating and streamlining approval processes within organizations. It addresses the class of problems related to manual, ad-hoc, and inefficient approval workflows, which can lead to delays, errors, and lack of transparency. The Approvals Connector aims to integrate with various systems and applications, enabling seamless and consistent approval processes across different departments and teams.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
The 101. Approvals Connector Overview is based on a modular and extensible architecture, allowing it to adapt to diverse organizational needs and requirements. At its core, the Approvals Connector facilitates the creation, management, and tracking of approval requests, ensuring that all stakeholders are informed and engaged throughout the process. This high-level mental model encompasses the key components, including approval templates, workflows, notifications, and analytics.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Approval Template | A pre-defined structure for creating approval requests, including fields, conditions, and workflows. |
| Approval Workflow | A series of steps and actions that define the approval process, including assignments, notifications, and deadlines. |
| Approval Request | A specific instance of an approval process, initiated by a user or system, which requires review and action by designated approvers. |
| Approver | An individual or group responsible for reviewing and acting upon an approval request. |
| Notification | An automated message or alert sent to stakeholders, informing them of approval requests, updates, or changes. |

## 4. Core Concepts
The fundamental ideas that form the basis of the 101. Approvals Connector Overview include:
* **Approval Templates**: reusable structures for creating approval requests
* **Approval Workflows**: customizable series of steps and actions for managing approval processes
* **Approval Requests**: individual instances of approval processes, initiated by users or systems
* **Approvers**: designated individuals or groups responsible for reviewing and acting upon approval requests
* **Notifications**: automated messages or alerts sent to stakeholders, ensuring transparency and engagement

## 5. Standard Model
The standard model for the 101. Approvals Connector Overview involves the following components and interactions:
1. **Approval Template Creation**: defining reusable approval templates with fields, conditions, and workflows
2. **Approval Request Initiation**: creating a new approval request based on a template, with relevant data and context
3. **Approval Workflow Execution**: managing the approval process, including assignments, notifications, and deadlines
4. **Approver Action**: reviewing and acting upon approval requests, with options for approval, rejection, or delegation
5. **Notification and Analytics**: sending automated messages and providing insights into approval processes and outcomes

## 6. Common Patterns
Recurring, accepted patterns in the 101. Approvals Connector Overview include:
* **Centralized Approval Management**: using a single, unified platform for managing all approval processes
* **Automated Workflow**: leveraging automated workflows to streamline approval processes and reduce manual errors
* **Conditional Approvals**: using conditional logic to route approval requests to specific approvers or workflows
* **Escalation and Delegation**: implementing escalation and delegation rules to ensure timely and effective approval decisions

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Common but discouraged practices in the 101. Approvals Connector Overview include:
* **Manual Approval Processes**: relying on manual, ad-hoc approval processes, which can lead to delays, errors, and lack of transparency
* **Insufficient Approval Templates**: using inadequate or inflexible approval templates, which can result in inefficient or ineffective approval processes
* **Inadequate Notification**: failing to provide timely and relevant notifications to stakeholders, which can lead to confusion, delays, or missed deadlines
* **Lack of Analytics**: neglecting to track and analyze approval processes and outcomes, which can hinder continuous improvement and optimization
