# 46. Posting to Activity Feed

Canonical documentation for 46. Posting to Activity Feed. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 46. Posting to Activity Feed exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of posting to an activity feed is to facilitate real-time information sharing, collaboration, and social interaction among users within a platform or application. The problem space addressed by this feature includes the need for users to share updates, achievements, or other relevant information with their network, and to stay informed about the activities of others. This feature is commonly found in social media platforms, online communities, and collaborative workspaces.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Posting to an activity feed involves creating and sharing a post, which can contain text, images, videos, or other media. The post is then displayed in a feed, which is a reverse-chronological list of all posts made by the user and others in their network. Users can interact with posts through likes, comments, and shares, allowing for engagement and discussion around the content.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Activity Feed | A reverse-chronological list of posts made by a user and others in their network. |
| Post | A single entry in the activity feed, containing text, images, videos, or other media. |
| User | An individual who creates and interacts with posts in the activity feed. |
| Network | The group of users who can see and interact with a user's posts. |
| Engagement | The act of interacting with a post through likes, comments, or shares. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of posting to an activity feed include:
* **Asynchronous communication**: Users can post and interact with content at any time, without requiring real-time presence.
* **Information sharing**: Posts can contain a wide range of information, from personal updates to news articles or educational content.
* **Social interaction**: Users can engage with each other's posts through likes, comments, and shares, fostering a sense of community and social connection.
* **Content discovery**: Users can discover new content and connect with others who share similar interests.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for posting to an activity feed involves the following steps:
1. **Post creation**: The user creates a new post, selecting the content and format they wish to share.
2. **Post submission**: The user submits the post, which is then added to their activity feed and the feeds of their network.
3. **Post display**: The post is displayed in the activity feed, along with any relevant metadata such as the user's name and profile picture.
4. **Engagement**: Users can interact with the post through likes, comments, and shares.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns for posting to an activity feed include:
* **Personal updates**: Users sharing personal news, achievements, or experiences.
* **Content curation**: Users sharing relevant and interesting content from other sources.
* **Discussion starters**: Users posting questions or prompts to spark discussion and engagement.
* **Event announcements**: Users sharing information about upcoming events or activities.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns for posting to an activity feed include:
* **Spamming**: Users posting repetitive or irrelevant content in an attempt to attract attention.
* **Self-promotion**: Users posting excessively about themselves or their own content.
* **Inflammatory content**: Users posting content that is intentionally provocative or offensive.
* **Overposting**: Users posting too frequently, flooding the feed and overwhelming their network.
