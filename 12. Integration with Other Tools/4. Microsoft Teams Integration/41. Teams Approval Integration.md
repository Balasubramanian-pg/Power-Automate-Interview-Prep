# 41. Teams Approval Integration

Canonical documentation for 41. Teams Approval Integration. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 41. Teams Approval Integration exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The Teams Approval Integration exists to streamline approval processes within Microsoft Teams, addressing the need for efficient and organized workflows. It targets the class of problems related to manual approval tracking, lack of transparency, and delayed decision-making, which can hinder team productivity and collaboration.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
The Teams Approval Integration is designed as a workflow automation tool that enables teams to create, manage, and track approvals directly within Microsoft Teams. It integrates with various services and applications, allowing for a unified and seamless approval experience. This integration aims to reduce the complexity and time associated with traditional approval processes, enhancing team efficiency and reducing errors.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Approval | A formal consent or validation required for a specific action, decision, or document. |
| Requester | The individual or entity initiating the approval request. |
| Approver | The person or group responsible for granting or denying approval. |
| Workflow | A series of activities or tasks that are automated or managed through the Teams Approval Integration. |
| Integration | The process of connecting the Teams Approval feature with other applications or services to enhance its functionality. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of the Teams Approval Integration include:
- **Automation**: Automating approval workflows to reduce manual effort and increase efficiency.
- **Customization**: Allowing users to tailor approval processes to meet specific team or organizational needs.
- **Integration**: Seamlessly connecting with other tools and services to provide a comprehensive approval experience.
- **Transparency**: Providing clear visibility into the approval process, including request status and history.
- **Security**: Ensuring that approvals are secure, auditable, and compliant with organizational policies.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for the Teams Approval Integration involves:
1. **Setup and Configuration**: Configuring the approval integration within Microsoft Teams, including setting up connectors to other services.
2. **Request Creation**: Creating approval requests, which can be triggered manually or through automated workflows.
3. **Approval Process**: Managing the approval workflow, including notifications, reminders, and escalation procedures.
4. **Decision and Notification**: Receiving and acting upon approval decisions, with notifications sent to relevant parties.
5. **Audit and Reporting**: Maintaining a record of all approval activities for auditing and reporting purposes.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in using the Teams Approval Integration include:
- **Ad Hoc Approvals**: Creating one-off approval requests for unique situations.
- **Recurring Approvals**: Setting up regular approval processes for routine decisions or actions.
- **Conditional Approvals**: Implementing approvals that are contingent upon specific conditions or criteria.
- **Multi-Stage Approvals**: Configuring approval workflows that require input from multiple levels or stakeholders.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns to avoid when using the Teams Approval Integration include:
- **Overly Complex Workflows**: Creating approval processes that are unnecessarily complicated or difficult to follow.
- **Insufficient Testing**: Failing to adequately test approval workflows before implementing them in a live environment.
- **Lack of Training**: Not providing sufficient training to users on how to effectively use the approval integration.
- **Inconsistent Use**: Failing to standardize the use of the approval integration across teams or departments.
