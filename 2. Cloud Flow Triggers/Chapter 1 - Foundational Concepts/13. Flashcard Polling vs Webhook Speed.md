## 5. Core Concepts

Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.

### 5.1 Polling Mechanism
The polling mechanism involves a client sending periodic requests to a server to retrieve updates to flashcard data. This approach can be simple to implement but may lead to increased server load and latency.

### 5.2 Webhook Notification
The webhook notification mechanism involves a server sending notifications to a client when updates to flashcard data occur. This approach can reduce server load and latency but may require more complex implementation and infrastructure.

### 5.3 Concept Interactions and Constraints
The choice between polling and webhooks depends on factors such as the frequency of updates, the number of clients, and the available system resources. Polling may be suitable for low-frequency updates, while webhooks may be more suitable for high-frequency updates. Additionally, webhooks may require additional infrastructure, such as message queues or notification services.
