# 37. Crafting Concise and Dynamic Approval Titles

Canonical documentation for 37. Crafting Concise and Dynamic Approval Titles. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 37. Crafting Concise and Dynamic Approval Titles exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of crafting concise and dynamic approval titles is to effectively communicate the purpose and context of an approval request, ensuring that approvers can quickly understand the request and make informed decisions. This addresses the problem of unclear or misleading approval titles, which can lead to delays, miscommunication, and incorrect approvals.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Crafting concise and dynamic approval titles involves considering the key elements of the approval request, such as the purpose, scope, and context, and distilling them into a clear and concise title. This title should be attention-grabbing, informative, and relevant to the approver, enabling them to quickly assess the request and take appropriate action.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Approval Title | A brief summary of the approval request, typically displayed in a workflow or approval system. |
| Concise | Brief and to the point, avoiding unnecessary words or complexity. |
| Dynamic | Adaptable to changing circumstances or contexts, such as updates to the approval request. |
| Approver | The individual or group responsible for reviewing and approving the request. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of crafting concise and dynamic approval titles include:
* Clarity: The title should clearly convey the purpose and context of the approval request.
* Brevity: The title should be brief and to the point, avoiding unnecessary words or complexity.
* Relevance: The title should be relevant to the approver, taking into account their role, responsibilities, and interests.
* Adaptability: The title should be adaptable to changing circumstances or contexts, such as updates to the approval request.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for crafting concise and dynamic approval titles involves:
1. Identifying the key elements of the approval request, such as the purpose, scope, and context.
2. Distilling these elements into a clear and concise title, using simple language and avoiding jargon or technical terms.
3. Reviewing and refining the title to ensure it is relevant, adaptable, and attention-grabbing.
4. Testing the title with approvers and stakeholders to ensure it meets their needs and expectations.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns for crafting concise and dynamic approval titles include:
* Using action verbs, such as "Approve" or "Review", to clearly convey the purpose of the request.
* Including relevant context, such as the project or initiative name, to help approvers understand the request.
* Using concise language and avoiding unnecessary words or complexity.
* Incorporating dynamic elements, such as variables or placeholders, to adapt to changing circumstances or contexts.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns for crafting concise and dynamic approval titles include:
* Using vague or generic titles, such as "Approval Request" or "Review Needed".
* Including unnecessary or irrelevant information, such as detailed technical specifications or background information.
* Using complex or technical language, which may confuse or intimidate approvers.
* Failing to review or refine the title, resulting in a title that is unclear, misleading, or outdated.
