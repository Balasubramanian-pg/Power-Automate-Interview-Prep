# 29. Logic of a Single Rejection in Everyone Mode

Canonical documentation for 29. Logic of a Single Rejection in Everyone Mode. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 29. Logic of a Single Rejection in Everyone Mode exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The Logic of a Single Rejection in Everyone Mode is designed to handle scenarios where a single rejection or veto can override collective decisions or agreements in a group setting. This concept is crucial in various domains, including decision-making processes, voting systems, and collaborative workflows, where the input of every individual matters, and a single dissenting voice can significantly impact the outcome.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
At its core, the Logic of a Single Rejection in Everyone Mode operates on the principle that any single entity or individual within a group has the power to reject a proposal, decision, or action, thereby preventing it from being implemented or accepted by the group as a whole. This model emphasizes the importance of consensus and the potential for any single member to act as a veto point.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Single Rejection | The act or power of one individual to reject a decision, thereby preventing its implementation. |
| Everyone Mode | A decision-making or operational mode where every individual's input or approval is required for a decision to be considered valid or accepted. |
| Veto Power | The authority or ability of an individual or entity to unilaterally reject a decision or proposal. |
| Consensus | A collective agreement or decision made by all members of a group. |
| Decision Threshold | The minimum level of agreement or approval required for a decision to be considered valid. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of the Logic of a Single Rejection in Everyone Mode include the principles of veto power, consensus decision-making, and the establishment of a decision threshold that requires unanimous agreement. These concepts are intertwined, as the presence of veto power implies a high decision threshold (unanimity) and underscores the importance of achieving consensus among all group members.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for implementing the Logic of a Single Rejection in Everyone Mode involves establishing clear guidelines and protocols for decision-making, ensuring that all members understand the implications of exercising veto power, and implementing mechanisms for conflict resolution or alternative decision paths when a rejection occurs. This model also emphasizes transparency, communication, and the fair representation of all members' interests.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns include the use of unanimous voting rules in critical decision-making processes, the designation of specific roles or positions with veto power, and the establishment of escalation procedures for resolving disputes or deadlocks resulting from a single rejection.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in the context of the Logic of a Single Rejection in Everyone Mode include the arbitrary or unjustified exercise of veto power, the lack of clear guidelines or transparency in decision-making processes, and the failure to establish effective conflict resolution mechanisms. These practices can lead to inefficiencies, mistrust, and ineffective decision-making.
