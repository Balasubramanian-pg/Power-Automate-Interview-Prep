# 119. Ensuring Compliance through Auditable Approval Loops

Canonical documentation for 119. Ensuring Compliance through Auditable Approval Loops. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 119. Ensuring Compliance through Auditable Approval Loops exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of 119. Ensuring Compliance through Auditable Approval Loops is to provide a structured approach to ensuring that all business processes and decisions are compliant with relevant laws, regulations, and internal policies. This is achieved by implementing auditable approval loops that track and record all approvals, rejections, and modifications made to a process or decision. The class of problems addressed by this topic includes non-compliance, lack of transparency, and inadequate accountability in business processes.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
The conceptual overview of 119. Ensuring Compliance through Auditable Approval Loops involves a multi-step process that includes initiation, review, approval, implementation, and auditing. Each step is designed to ensure that all business processes and decisions are thoroughly reviewed, approved, and documented, with a clear audit trail to demonstrate compliance.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Approval Loop | A series of steps that require approval or rejection by authorized personnel before a process or decision can be implemented. |
| Auditable Record | A permanent and unalterable record of all approvals, rejections, and modifications made to a process or decision. |
| Compliance | Adherence to relevant laws, regulations, and internal policies. |
| Decision Maker | An individual with the authority to approve or reject a process or decision. |
| Stakeholder | An individual or group with an interest in the outcome of a process or decision. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of 119. Ensuring Compliance through Auditable Approval Loops include:
* Segregation of duties: ensuring that no single individual has control over an entire process or decision.
* Dual control: requiring two or more authorized personnel to approve or reject a process or decision.
* Audit trails: maintaining a permanent and unalterable record of all approvals, rejections, and modifications.
* Transparency: ensuring that all stakeholders have access to relevant information and can track the progress of a process or decision.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for 119. Ensuring Compliance through Auditable Approval Loops involves the following steps:
1. Initiation: a request is made to initiate a process or decision.
2. Review: the request is reviewed by authorized personnel to ensure compliance with relevant laws, regulations, and internal policies.
3. Approval: the request is approved or rejected by authorized personnel.
4. Implementation: the approved process or decision is implemented.
5. Auditing: the process or decision is audited to ensure compliance and to identify areas for improvement.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns for 119. Ensuring Compliance through Auditable Approval Loops include:
* Using workflow management systems to automate and track approval loops.
* Implementing role-based access control to ensure that only authorized personnel can approve or reject a process or decision.
* Conducting regular audits to ensure compliance and identify areas for improvement.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns for 119. Ensuring Compliance through Auditable Approval Loops include:
* Allowing a single individual to have control over an entire process or decision.
* Failing to maintain a permanent and unalterable record of approvals, rejections, and modifications.
* Ignoring or bypassing approval loops in order to expedite a process or decision.
