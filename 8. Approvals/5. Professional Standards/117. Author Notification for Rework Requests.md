# 117. Author Notification for Rework Requests

Canonical documentation for 117. Author Notification for Rework Requests. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 117. Author Notification for Rework Requests exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of Author Notification for Rework Requests is to provide a standardized mechanism for notifying authors of the need for rework or revisions to their submissions, addressing the problem of unclear or inconsistent communication between authors, reviewers, and editors. This process aims to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the review and revision cycle, reducing delays and misunderstandings.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Author Notification for Rework Requests involves a structured process where editors or reviewers identify areas requiring improvement in a submission and notify the author(s) with specific, actionable feedback. This notification typically includes a clear description of the required rework, a deadline for completion, and contact information for the editor or reviewer. The author then revises the submission according to the feedback and resubmits it for further review.

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of Author Notification for Rework Requests include:
- **Clarity and Specificity**: Feedback must be clear, specific, and actionable to enable authors to understand and address the required changes.
- **Timeliness**: Notifications should be sent in a timely manner to allow authors sufficient time to complete the requested rework.
- **Transparency**: The process should be transparent, with clear communication about the reasons for the rework request, the expected outcomes, and the next steps.
- **Collaboration**: The rework request process should facilitate collaboration between authors, editors, and reviewers to improve the quality of the submission.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for Author Notification for Rework Requests typically involves the following steps:
1. **Review**: The submission is reviewed by editors or reviewers who identify areas requiring improvement.
2. **Feedback Compilation**: The feedback is compiled into a clear, concise document or message.
3. **Notification**: The author is notified of the need for rework, including the specific feedback and any deadlines.
4. **Revision**: The author revises the submission according to the feedback.
5. **Resubmission**: The revised submission is resubmitted for further review.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in Author Notification for Rework Requests include:
- **Iterative Review**: Multiple rounds of review and revision to achieve the desired quality.
- **Template-based Feedback**: Using standardized templates to provide feedback, ensuring consistency and clarity.
- **Priority Feedback**: Identifying and communicating the most critical areas for improvement first.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns to avoid in Author Notification for Rework Requests include:
- **Vague Feedback**: Providing feedback that is too general or vague to be actionable.
- **Unrealistic Deadlines**: Setting deadlines that are too tight for authors to complete the requested rework.
- **Lack of Transparency**: Failing to clearly communicate the reasons for the rework request or the expected outcomes.
