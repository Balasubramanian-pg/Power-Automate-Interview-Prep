# 122. Managing Approval Reassignments Manually

Canonical documentation for 122. Managing Approval Reassignments Manually. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 122. Managing Approval Reassignments Manually exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of managing approval reassignments manually is to provide a systematic approach to reassigning approvals in situations where the original approver is unavailable or unable to perform their duties. This addresses the problem of delayed or stalled workflows due to approver unavailability, ensuring that business processes continue uninterrupted.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Managing approval reassignments manually involves a series of steps that allow organizations to efficiently reassign approval tasks to alternative approvers. This process typically includes identifying the need for reassignment, selecting a new approver, and updating the approval workflow to reflect the change.

| Workflow | A series of tasks or processes that are completed in a specific order to achieve a particular goal. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of managing approval reassignments manually include:
- **Identification of Need**: Recognizing when an approval reassignment is necessary.
- **Approver Selection**: Choosing an appropriate alternative approver.
- **Workflow Update**: Modifying the approval workflow to reflect the reassignment.
- **Notification**: Informing relevant parties of the reassignment.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for managing approval reassignments manually typically involves the following steps:
1. **Initiation**: Identifying the need for approval reassignment.
2. **Selection**: Choosing a new approver based on predefined criteria.
3. **Reassignment**: Updating the approval workflow to reflect the change.
4. **Notification**: Notifying the new approver and other relevant parties.
5. **Verification**: Confirming that the reassignment has been successfully completed.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in managing approval reassignments manually include:
- **Temporary Reassignment**: Reassigning approvals temporarily due to an approver's absence.
- **Permanent Reassignment**: Reassigning approvals permanently due to a change in roles or responsibilities.
- **Escalation**: Automatically reassigning approvals to a higher authority after a certain period.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in managing approval reassignments manually include:
- **Lack of Documentation**: Failing to document reassignments, leading to confusion and potential errors.
- **Inconsistent Selection**: Applying inconsistent criteria for selecting new approvers.
- **Insufficient Notification**: Failing to notify relevant parties of reassignments, leading to misunderstandings and delays.
