# 110. Notifying Flow Owners of Overdue Tasks

Canonical documentation for 110. Notifying Flow Owners of Overdue Tasks. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 110. Notifying Flow Owners of Overdue Tasks exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of notifying flow owners of overdue tasks is to ensure timely completion of tasks and maintain a smooth workflow. The problem space includes delayed task completion, lack of visibility into task status, and inadequate communication among team members. This feature addresses these issues by providing a mechanism for automatic notification of flow owners when tasks become overdue.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
The conceptual overview of notifying flow owners of overdue tasks involves a workflow management system that tracks task assignments, deadlines, and completion status. When a task becomes overdue, the system triggers a notification to the flow owner, alerting them to take necessary actions to get the task back on track. This overview encompasses the key components of task management, notification mechanisms, and workflow ownership.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Flow Owner | The individual responsible for overseeing the workflow and ensuring tasks are completed on time. |
| Overdue Task | A task that has not been completed by its designated deadline. |
| Notification | An alert or message sent to the flow owner when a task becomes overdue. |
| Workflow | A series of tasks that are interconnected and contribute to a larger process or project. |
| Task Assignment | The process of designating a task to a specific individual or team. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of notifying flow owners of overdue tasks include:
* Task management: The process of creating, assigning, and tracking tasks within a workflow.
* Deadline management: The process of setting and managing deadlines for tasks to ensure timely completion.
* Notification mechanisms: The methods used to alert flow owners of overdue tasks, such as email, in-app notifications, or SMS.
* Workflow ownership: The responsibility of overseeing the workflow and ensuring tasks are completed on time.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for notifying flow owners of overdue tasks involves the following steps:
1. Task assignment: Tasks are assigned to team members with designated deadlines.
2. Deadline tracking: The workflow management system tracks the deadlines and completion status of tasks.
3. Overdue task detection: The system detects when a task becomes overdue.
4. Notification triggering: The system triggers a notification to the flow owner when a task becomes overdue.
5. Flow owner action: The flow owner takes necessary actions to get the task back on track, such as reassigning the task or adjusting the deadline.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns for notifying flow owners of overdue tasks include:
* Daily or weekly summary reports of overdue tasks.
* Customizable notification preferences, such as email or in-app notifications.
* Escalation procedures for tasks that remain overdue after a specified period.
* Integration with calendar systems to schedule reminders and deadlines.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns for notifying flow owners of overdue tasks include:
* Not providing clear deadlines or expectations for task completion.
* Failing to track or monitor task progress.
* Notifying flow owners too frequently or with excessive detail, leading to notification fatigue.
* Not providing a clear escalation procedure for overdue tasks.
