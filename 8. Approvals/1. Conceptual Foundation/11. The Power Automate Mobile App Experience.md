# 11. The Power Automate Mobile App Experience

Canonical documentation for 11. The Power Automate Mobile App Experience. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 11. The Power Automate Mobile App Experience exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The Power Automate Mobile App Experience exists to provide users with a seamless and intuitive way to automate tasks and workflows on-the-go. It addresses the problem of limited mobility and accessibility in traditional automation tools, allowing users to manage and monitor their automated workflows from anywhere, at any time. This experience is designed to cater to the growing demand for mobile accessibility and flexibility in the modern workforce.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
The Power Automate Mobile App Experience is built around the concept of providing a unified, user-friendly interface for managing automated workflows, approvals, and notifications. It integrates with various services and applications, enabling users to automate tasks, receive notifications, and take actions directly from their mobile devices. The experience is designed to be intuitive, with a simple and consistent navigation, making it easy for users to find and use the features they need.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Automation | The process of automating repetitive or mundane tasks using predefined rules and workflows. |
| Workflow | A series of automated tasks or actions that are triggered by specific events or conditions. |
| Approval | A process of reviewing and authorizing or rejecting a request or action. |
| Notification | A message or alert sent to a user to inform them of an event or update. |
| Mobile App | A software application designed to run on mobile devices, providing a specific set of features and functionalities. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of the Power Automate Mobile App Experience include:
* Automation: The ability to automate repetitive or mundane tasks using predefined rules and workflows.
* Integration: The ability to integrate with various services and applications, enabling users to automate tasks and receive notifications.
* Mobility: The ability to access and manage automated workflows, approvals, and notifications from anywhere, at any time.
* User Experience: The design and functionality of the mobile app, providing an intuitive and user-friendly interface for managing automated workflows and tasks.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for the Power Automate Mobile App Experience includes the following components:
* Dashboard: A centralized hub for viewing and managing automated workflows, approvals, and notifications.
* Workflow Management: The ability to create, edit, and manage automated workflows, including setting up triggers, actions, and conditions.
* Approval Management: The ability to manage and track approvals, including receiving notifications and taking actions.
* Notification Management: The ability to manage and customize notifications, including setting up notification rules and preferences.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in the Power Automate Mobile App Experience include:
* Using automated workflows to streamline repetitive tasks and processes.
* Integrating with other services and applications to expand automation capabilities.
* Customizing notification preferences to receive alerts and updates on specific events or actions.
* Using the mobile app to manage and monitor automated workflows, approvals, and notifications on-the-go.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in the Power Automate Mobile App Experience include:
* Over-automating tasks and workflows, leading to complexity and maintenance issues.
* Failing to test and validate automated workflows, leading to errors and unexpected behavior.
* Ignoring security and access controls, leading to unauthorized access or data breaches.
* Not providing adequate training or support, leading to user confusion and adoption issues.
