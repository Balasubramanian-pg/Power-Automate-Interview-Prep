# 23. The Teams Approvals App Dashboard

Canonical documentation for 23. The Teams Approvals App Dashboard. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 23. The Teams Approvals App Dashboard exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The Teams Approvals App Dashboard is designed to streamline approval processes within Microsoft Teams, providing a centralized interface for managing and tracking approvals. It addresses the challenges of manual approval processes, such as lost or misplaced requests, delayed responses, and lack of visibility into approval status. By leveraging the Teams Approvals App Dashboard, organizations can automate and simplify approval workflows, reducing the time and effort required to obtain approvals and improving overall productivity.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
The Teams Approvals App Dashboard is a user-friendly interface that enables users to create, manage, and track approval requests within Microsoft Teams. It integrates with various Teams features, such as channels, chats, and files, to provide a seamless approval experience. The dashboard offers a range of features, including customizable approval templates, automated reminders, and real-time tracking, to facilitate efficient and transparent approval processes.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Approval Request | A formal request for approval, which can be created and submitted through the Teams Approvals App Dashboard. |
| Approval Template | A pre-defined template used to create approval requests, which can be customized to meet specific business needs. |
| Approver | An individual or group designated to review and approve or reject an approval request. |
| Dashboard | The central interface of the Teams Approvals App, where users can create, manage, and track approval requests. |
| Notification | An automated alert sent to approvers and requesters to inform them of approval request status updates. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The Teams Approvals App Dashboard is built around several core concepts, including:
* **Approval Workflows**: The automated processes that govern the approval request lifecycle, from creation to completion.
* **Customization**: The ability to tailor approval templates and workflows to meet specific business requirements.
* **Integration**: The seamless interaction between the Teams Approvals App Dashboard and other Microsoft Teams features.
* **Real-time Tracking**: The ability to monitor approval request status in real-time, enabling prompt follow-up and decision-making.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for the Teams Approvals App Dashboard involves the following components:
* **Approval Request Creation**: Users create and submit approval requests through the dashboard.
* **Approval Workflow Automation**: The approval request is routed to designated approvers, who receive notifications and can review the request.
* **Approval or Rejection**: Approvers review the request and provide a decision, which is recorded and updated in the dashboard.
* **Notification and Follow-up**: Automated notifications inform requesters and approvers of approval request status updates.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns observed in the use of the Teams Approvals App Dashboard include:
* **Centralized Approval Management**: Organizations use the dashboard as a single point of truth for managing and tracking approval requests.
* **Customized Approval Workflows**: Businesses tailor approval workflows to meet specific requirements, such as multi-level approvals or conditional logic.
* **Integration with Other Teams Features**: Users leverage the dashboard's integration with other Microsoft Teams features, such as channels and files, to streamline approval processes.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns to avoid when using the Teams Approvals App Dashboard include:
* **Manual Approval Processes**: Relying on manual methods, such as email or paper-based approvals, which can lead to delays and errors.
* **Insufficient Customization**: Failing to tailor approval templates and workflows to meet specific business needs, resulting in inefficient or ineffective approval processes.
* **Lack of Training**: Not providing adequate training to users, which can lead to confusion and misuse of the dashboard.
