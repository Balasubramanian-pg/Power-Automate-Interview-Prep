# 80. The Wait for an Approval Action Requirements

Canonical documentation for 80. The Wait for an Approval Action Requirements. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 80. The Wait for an Approval Action Requirements exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The Wait for an Approval Action Requirements exists to provide a standardized framework for managing approval workflows in business processes. It addresses the class of problems related to inefficient approval processes, lack of transparency, and inadequate control over approval workflows. This includes issues such as delayed approvals, lost or misplaced requests, and insufficient audit trails.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
The Wait for an Approval Action Requirements can be conceptualized as a state machine that manages the approval workflow. It consists of three primary states: Pending, Approved, and Rejected. The workflow transitions between these states based on user interactions, such as submitting a request, approving or rejecting a request, and canceling a pending request.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Approval Workflow | A sequence of tasks and decisions that lead to the approval or rejection of a request. |
| Requester | The individual or entity that submits a request for approval. |
| Approver | The individual or entity responsible for reviewing and approving or rejecting a request. |
| Approval Threshold | The minimum number of approvals required for a request to be considered approved. |
| Escalation Policy | A set of rules that define the actions to be taken when a request is not approved or rejected within a specified timeframe. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of the Wait for an Approval Action Requirements include:
* **Approval Workflow Management**: The ability to define, execute, and manage approval workflows.
* **Request and Approval Tracking**: The ability to track the status of requests and approvals.
* **Notification and Escalation**: The ability to notify approvers and requesters of pending requests and escalate requests that are not approved or rejected within a specified timeframe.
* **Audit and Compliance**: The ability to maintain an audit trail of all approval-related activities and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for the Wait for an Approval Action Requirements consists of the following components:
* **Request Submission**: The requester submits a request for approval.
* **Approval Workflow Execution**: The approval workflow is executed, and the request is routed to the approver.
* **Approval or Rejection**: The approver reviews the request and approves or rejects it.
* **Notification and Escalation**: The requester and approver are notified of the approval or rejection, and the request is escalated if necessary.
* **Audit and Compliance**: The approval-related activities are audited, and compliance with regulatory requirements is ensured.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns for the Wait for an Approval Action Requirements include:
* **Serial Approval**: A single approver reviews and approves or rejects a request.
* **Parallel Approval**: Multiple approvers review and approve or reject a request simultaneously.
* **Hierarchical Approval**: Requests are approved or rejected by multiple approvers in a hierarchical sequence.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns for the Wait for an Approval Action Requirements include:
* **Manual Approval Processes**: Using manual processes, such as paper-based forms or email, to manage approval workflows.
* **Lack of Transparency**: Failing to provide clear visibility into the approval workflow and request status.
* **Inadequate Audit Trails**: Failing to maintain adequate audit trails of approval-related activities.
