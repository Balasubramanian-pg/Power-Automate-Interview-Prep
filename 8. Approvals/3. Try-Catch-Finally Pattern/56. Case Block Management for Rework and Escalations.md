# 56. Case Block Management for Rework and Escalations

Canonical documentation for 56. Case Block Management for Rework and Escalations. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 56. Case Block Management for Rework and Escalations exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of Case Block Management for Rework and Escalations is to provide a structured approach to managing cases that require rework or escalation, ensuring that issues are resolved efficiently and effectively. This approach addresses the class of problems related to case management, including delayed resolutions, incomplete information, and lack of transparency. By implementing Case Block Management, organizations can improve their overall case management process, reduce cycle times, and enhance customer satisfaction.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Case Block Management for Rework and Escalations involves a systematic process for identifying, categorizing, and prioritizing cases that require rework or escalation. This process includes assessing the case complexity, assigning the appropriate resources, and monitoring progress to ensure timely resolution. The conceptual overview of Case Block Management involves the following key components: case identification, case assessment, case prioritization, resource allocation, and progress monitoring.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Case | A specific issue or problem that requires resolution |
| Rework | The process of revisiting and revising a case to ensure accuracy and completeness |
| Escalation | The process of elevating a case to a higher level of authority or expertise for resolution |
| Case Block | A group of cases that share similar characteristics or requirements |
| Case Manager | The individual responsible for overseeing the case management process |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of Case Block Management for Rework and Escalations include:
* Case assessment: evaluating the complexity and requirements of each case
* Case prioritization: assigning priority levels to cases based on their urgency and importance
* Resource allocation: assigning the necessary resources, including personnel and equipment, to resolve cases
* Progress monitoring: tracking the status and progress of each case to ensure timely resolution
* Continuous improvement: identifying areas for improvement and implementing changes to optimize the case management process

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for Case Block Management for Rework and Escalations involves the following steps:
1. Case identification and assessment
2. Case prioritization and categorization
3. Resource allocation and assignment
4. Progress monitoring and tracking
5. Continuous improvement and feedback

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in Case Block Management for Rework and Escalations include:
* Using a tiered approach to case management, with multiple levels of authority and expertise
* Implementing a case management system to track and monitor case progress
* Establishing clear communication channels and protocols for case escalation
* Providing ongoing training and support for case managers and other stakeholders

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in Case Block Management for Rework and Escalations include:
* Failing to establish clear case management processes and protocols
* Insufficiently training or supporting case managers and other stakeholders
* Not prioritizing cases based on their urgency and importance
* Failing to monitor and track case progress, leading to delays and inefficiencies
