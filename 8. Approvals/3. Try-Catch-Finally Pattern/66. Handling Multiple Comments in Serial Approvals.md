# 66. Handling Multiple Comments in Serial Approvals

Canonical documentation for 66. Handling Multiple Comments in Serial Approvals. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 66. Handling Multiple Comments in Serial Approvals exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of handling multiple comments in serial approvals is to facilitate efficient and organized communication among stakeholders in a sequential approval process. This addresses the problem of managing and tracking multiple comments, suggestions, and feedback from various stakeholders, ensuring that all concerns are addressed and approvals are granted in a timely manner.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Handling multiple comments in serial approvals involves a structured approach to collecting, reviewing, and responding to comments from stakeholders. This process ensures that all comments are acknowledged, addressed, and resolved in a sequential and transparent manner, ultimately leading to informed decision-making and efficient approvals.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Serial Approval | A sequential approval process where stakeholders review and approve a document or request in a predetermined order. |
| Comment | A remark, suggestion, or feedback provided by a stakeholder during the approval process. |
| Stakeholder | An individual or group with an interest or concern in the approval process. |
| Approval Workflow | A predefined sequence of steps and stakeholders involved in the approval process. |
| Comment Thread | A series of related comments and responses exchanged among stakeholders. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of handling multiple comments in serial approvals include:
* Comment collection and tracking
* Stakeholder management and notification
* Sequential review and approval
* Transparent communication and feedback
* Version control and audit trails

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for handling multiple comments in serial approvals involves the following steps:
1. Initialize the approval workflow and notify stakeholders.
2. Collect and track comments from stakeholders.
3. Review and respond to comments in a sequential manner.
4. Update the document or request based on stakeholder feedback.
5. Obtain approvals from stakeholders in the predetermined order.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in handling multiple comments in serial approvals include:
* Using a centralized comment tracking system
* Establishing clear communication channels and protocols
* Implementing a version control system to manage document updates
* Conducting regular stakeholder meetings and status updates
* Maintaining a transparent and auditable approval process

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in handling multiple comments in serial approvals include:
* Using email or informal communication channels for comment tracking
* Failing to establish a clear approval workflow or stakeholder roles
* Ignoring or dismissing stakeholder comments without feedback
* Making unauthorized changes to documents or requests
* Lacking transparency and accountability in the approval process
