## 2. Conceptual Overview

The conceptual overview of 67. Logic for Response vs Outcome in Arrays involves understanding the distinction between response and outcome logic. Response logic refers to the immediate output or reaction of a system to a given input, whereas outcome logic refers to the overall result or effect of a series of operations. In the context of arrays, this means considering how array operations such as sorting, filtering, and mapping affect the final outcome.
