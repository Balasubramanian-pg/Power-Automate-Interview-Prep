# 71. Formatting Consolidated Comment Lists

Canonical documentation for 71. Formatting Consolidated Comment Lists. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 71. Formatting Consolidated Comment Lists exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of formatting consolidated comment lists is to provide a standardized approach to organizing and displaying comments from multiple sources, making it easier to review, analyze, and respond to feedback. This addresses the problem of information overload and disorganization that can occur when dealing with large numbers of comments from various stakeholders.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Formatting consolidated comment lists involves collecting comments from different sources, categorizing and prioritizing them, and presenting them in a clear and concise manner. This process enables effective communication, collaboration, and decision-making by providing a comprehensive and organized view of all relevant comments.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Consolidated Comment List | A collection of comments from multiple sources, organized and formatted for easy review and analysis. |
| Comment | A remark, suggestion, or opinion expressed by a stakeholder, typically in response to a document, proposal, or idea. |
| Stakeholder | An individual or group with an interest or concern in a particular project, document, or decision. |
| Categorization | The process of grouping comments into categories based on their content, theme, or relevance. |
| Prioritization | The process of assigning a level of importance or urgency to comments, determining which ones to address first. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of formatting consolidated comment lists include:
* Data collection: gathering comments from various sources
* Data categorization: grouping comments into meaningful categories
* Data prioritization: assigning importance and urgency to comments
* Data visualization: presenting comments in a clear and concise format
* Collaboration: facilitating communication and decision-making among stakeholders

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for formatting consolidated comment lists involves the following steps:
1. Collect comments from all relevant sources
2. Categorize comments based on their content or theme
3. Prioritize comments based on their importance and urgency
4. Format comments into a clear and concise list or table
5. Review and analyze the consolidated comment list
6. Respond to and address comments as necessary

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in formatting consolidated comment lists include:
* Using tables or spreadsheets to organize and display comments
* Categorizing comments by theme, such as "technical" or "editorial"
* Prioritizing comments based on their impact or urgency
* Using color-coding or highlighting to draw attention to important comments
* Including a summary or overview of the comments at the beginning of the list

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in formatting consolidated comment lists include:
* Failing to collect comments from all relevant sources
* Not categorizing or prioritizing comments, making it difficult to review and analyze them
* Using a disorganized or unclear format, making it hard to understand the comments
* Ignoring or dismissing comments without proper consideration
* Not responding to or addressing comments in a timely manner
